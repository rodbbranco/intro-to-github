How it all started

Rodrigo's journey to become a front-end developer started with a project with his dad.
His dad, a seasoned full-stack developer, was always complaining about how there was a shortage of good developers in the market.
Rodrigo, always eager to make his dad proud, decided to take up the development.
Little did he know, he was in for a wild ride.

His first encounter with HTML was an epic disaster.
He thought HTML stood for "How to Make Lasagna."
He was so confident in his newfound knowledge that he decided to make a website about lasagna.
His dad was mortified when he saw the website, but to Rodrigo's surprise, his dad was impressed by his creativity.

From that day on, Rodrigo's dad took on the role of a mentor, guiding him through the world of development.
Rodrigo learned about HTML tags, CSS styles, and JavaScript functions, but he never forgot his love for lasagna.
He created a website about the history of lasagna and even added a recipe section.

Rodrigo's dad was proud of his son's progress, but he couldn't help but tease him about his love for lasagna.
Whenever Rodrigo got stuck on a coding problem, his dad would say, "Just add a little more tomato sauce, and it will all come together."

In the end, Rodrigo became a skilled front-end developer and was even hired by a top tech company.
He never forgot his roots and still had a special place in his heart for lasagna.
Whenever he needed a break from coding, he would whip up a batch of lasagna, and his coworkers would gather around his desk, mesmerized by the delicious aroma.

The story of Rodrigo, the lasagna-loving front-end developer, is a reminder that everyone has to start somewhere,
and sometimes, it's the quirky things that make us unique and set us apart from the rest.
